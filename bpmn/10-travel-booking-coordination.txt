Travel Booking Coordination (Collaboration)

Background:
A travel agency arranges trips for travelers. To confirm a trip, the agency talks to
two independent systems: an airline reservation system and a hotel booking system.
Both systems are operated by other companies. This is a collaboration between several
participants that exchange messages.

Goal: Please model the following as a multi-participant BPMN collaboration.
List of participants (pools): Traveler, Travel Agency, Airline, Hotel.
The Travel Agency is the pool we control and must be a white box with its internal
process. The Traveler is an external party modeled as a black-box (empty) pool. The
Airline and the Hotel are independent external systems and are also modeled as
black-box pools — we only send requests and receive responses from them.

The collaboration: the Traveler sends a trip request to the Travel Agency. An agent
opens the request and, at the same time, sends a seat-availability request to the
Airline and a room-availability request to the Hotel. When both the Airline and the
Hotel reply with availability, the agent assembles a trip offer and sends it to the
Traveler. If the Traveler declines the offer, the agent cancels the draft bookings and
the process ends. If the Traveler accepts, the agent sends a booking confirmation to
the Airline and a booking confirmation to the Hotel, then sends a final itinerary
message to the Traveler, and the process ends.

Each interaction between the Travel Agency and the Traveler, the Airline, or the Hotel
is a message flow (dashed), since information passes between separate organizations.
The Travel Agency's own steps (open request, assemble offer, cancel draft bookings)
are linked by sequence flows and remain inside the Travel Agency pool. Note as an
annotation: assume the Airline and Hotel always respond, so no timeout handling is
modeled for their replies.
