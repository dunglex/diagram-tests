Online Order Fulfillment (Collaboration)

Background:
An online retailer sells goods through its web shop. The order is paid through an
external payment provider and shipped by an independent courier company. These are
separate organizations that exchange messages with the shop.

Goal: Please model the following as a multi-participant BPMN collaboration.
There are four participants (pools): Customer, Online Shop, Payment Provider, Courier.
Only the Online Shop is a process we control (white box). The Customer, the Payment
Provider, and the Courier are external parties and should be modeled as black-box
pools (empty pools) — we do not control their internal steps, we only exchange
messages with them.

The collaboration works like this. The Customer sends an order to the Online Shop.
When the shop receives the order, it checks stock availability. If the item is out of
stock, the shop sends an "out of stock" notice back to the Customer and the process
ends. If the item is in stock, the shop sends a payment request to the Payment
Provider and waits. When the Payment Provider returns a payment confirmation, the shop
picks and packs the goods, then hands the parcel to the Courier by sending a shipment
request. Once the parcel is handed over, the shop sends an order-confirmation message
to the Customer and the process ends.

All exchanges between the Online Shop and the Customer, the Payment Provider, and the
Courier are message flows (dashed), because information changes hands between separate
organizations. The internal steps of the Online Shop (check stock, pick and pack) are
connected by sequence flows and stay entirely within the Online Shop pool.
