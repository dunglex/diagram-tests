Loan Application Review

Background:
A regional bank processes personal loan applications. Each application must pass a
credit check and a document verification step before a loan officer can decide, and
larger loans additionally require manager sign-off.

Goal: Please model the following process.
Use 4 lanes: Applicant, Loan Officer, Credit Analyst, Branch Manager.

An applicant submits a loan application. The loan officer receives it and opens a case
file. From there, two things happen at the same time: the credit analyst pulls a credit
report and scores the applicant, while the loan officer independently verifies the
submitted documents (proof of income, ID, address). Once both the credit score and the
document verification are complete, the loan officer reviews the combined results.

If the requested amount is small, the loan officer can approve or reject it directly.
If the requested amount is large, the case is forwarded to the branch manager, who
gives the final approval or rejection. Whichever path is taken, an approved or rejected
decision converges back to a single "record decision" step handled by the loan officer.

If the loan officer notices that a required document is missing or unreadable during
verification, they send the application back to the applicant with a request for the
missing document; once the applicant resubmits, verification is repeated until the
documents are complete.

When the branch manager gives final approval on a large loan, that approval is sent
directly to the archiving clerk role (modeled as a task in the Loan Officer lane) to
close the file, skipping the intermediate credit-analyst and document-check steps
since they were already completed earlier in the process.

Note as an annotation: assume the credit report is always available within the same
business day, so no timeout handling is modeled for the credit check step.
