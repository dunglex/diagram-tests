Expense Reimbursement

Background:
Employees at a mid-size company submit expense reports that must be approved by
their manager and then processed by finance before reimbursement is paid out.

Goal: Please model the following process.
Use 3 lanes: Employee, Manager, Finance.

An employee fills out an expense report and submits it with receipts attached. The
manager reviews the report. If the manager rejects it (for example, missing receipts or
policy violations), the report is sent back to the employee with comments; the employee
corrects the report and resubmits it, and the manager reviews it again. This can repeat
until the manager approves.

Once the manager approves, the report goes to finance. Finance checks whether the
report is under the standard reimbursement threshold or over it. If it's under the
threshold, finance processes payment directly. If it's over the threshold, finance
routes it to the manager for a second, higher-level approval before payment is
processed. Both the direct-payment path and the second-approval path converge back to
a single "process payment" step in the Finance lane.

After payment is processed, finance sends a confirmation directly back to the employee,
letting them know the reimbursement has been paid.

Note as an annotation: assume all currency amounts are already in the company's home
currency, so no currency-conversion step is modeled.
