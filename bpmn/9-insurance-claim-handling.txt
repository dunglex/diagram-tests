Insurance Claim Handling (Collaboration)

Background:
A car insurance company handles damage claims. When a customer reports damage, the
insurer coordinates with an independent repair garage to fix the vehicle. The customer
and the garage are separate parties that communicate with the insurer by exchanging
messages.

Goal: Please model the following as a multi-participant BPMN collaboration.
The participants (pools) are: Policyholder, Insurance Company, Repair Garage.
The Insurance Company is the process we control and must be a white-box pool with its
full internal flow. The Policyholder is an external party and should be a black-box
(empty) pool. The Repair Garage is also modeled as a black-box pool — we send it work
and receive an outcome, but we do not model its internal repair steps.

The flow: the Policyholder submits a claim to the Insurance Company. A claims handler
registers the claim and then verifies whether the policy is valid. If the policy is
not valid, the handler sends a rejection message to the Policyholder and the process
ends. If the policy is valid, the handler assesses the damage. If the estimated cost
is above the threshold, an assessor must approve the claim before continuing;
otherwise it continues directly. Once approved (or if approval was not required), the
Insurance Company sends a repair order to the Repair Garage and waits. When the Repair
Garage returns a repair-completed message, the handler releases the payment and sends a
settlement notice to the Policyholder, and the process ends.

Every hand-off between the Insurance Company and the Policyholder or the Repair Garage
is a message flow (dashed). The internal steps inside the Insurance Company (register
claim, verify policy, assess damage, approve, release payment) are connected by
sequence flows and must not cross the pool boundary.
